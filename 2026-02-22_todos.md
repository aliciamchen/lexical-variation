# Observations and TODOs, February 22 2026

Experiment changes after running one pilot with `refer + mixed` and `social + mixed` conditions 

## Observations

### Recruitment

- For `social + mixed` run: 20 filled out survey -> 17 said they can make it on survey -> 12 people confirm to message -> we send to everyone that said they make it on survey -> 17 people log on to the experiment -> 9 people start the experiment -> 1 person times out -> 8 people finish the experiment
- For `refer + mixed` run: 26 filled out first survey -> 23 said they can make it on survey -> XXX confirmed (need to count) -> 18 logged in -> 9 people start the experiment, all finish. 
  - For this run, I tried making a new batch with one `refer + separated` game (because it was originally designed to be able to collect two games, although in retrospection this was tight), but i made it after some participants were already assigned to the other batch, and once that game started those participants got kicked. It turns out that Empirica cannot move players between games if they are different treatments. So we should pre-make all the batches, and set `preferUnderassignedGames: true` (more details in the empirica documentation: https://docs.empirica.ly/overview/randomization-and-batches)
